About the leafs:

The Toronto Maple Leafs are a professional ice hockey team based in Toronto.
They compete in the NHL(National Hockey League as a member of the Atlantic Division
in the Eastern Conference. The team was founded in 1917, operating simply as Toronto and
known then as the Toronto Arenas. In 1919 they were renamed the Toronto St.Patricks then
again in 1927 to Toronto Maple Leafs.

The team is currently affiliated with two minor league teams: the Toronto Marlies of the
American Hockey League and the Newfoundland Growlers of the ECHL.

Accomplishments:

  • 13 Stanley Cups
      - The team won 1 cup as the Toronto Arenas, and the other
      12 as the Toronto Maple Leafs with their most recent in 1966.
  • 6 Division Championships

  • Several Hall of Fame inductees, notabaly: Tim Horton, Terry Sawchuk, Ed Belfour and Mats Sundin

    • Mats Sundin remains the most iconic Maple Leaf to ever wear the famous blue and white.

    - Mats Sundin was drafted 1st overall in the 1989 by the then Quebec Nordiques and then traded
    to the Maple Leafs in the 1994, where he played the majority of his career, also serving 11
    seasons at their captain! A dynamic playmaker using his skill and size to help him be a prolific
    player in his career, Mats accumulated atleast 70 points in every season of his career. Although
    he never won a cup with the Leafs, he will always have a lasting impression with the franchise.

    "I'm very proud to be wearing the "C" for the Maple Leafs. It puts a smile on my face everyday."
